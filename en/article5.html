The quote: "You're a wizard, Harry”

It was in 1997 that J.K. Rowling unveiled a world of magic with Harry Potter. The line "You're a wizard, Harry" symbolises the hero's initiation, becoming a timeless emblem of this captivating saga.

By COLLIN Hugo
Published on 18 October 2023

Bewitched

1997, the year we discovered a world of magic and mystery with the Harry Potter stories. The world of literature was enchanted by the arrival of a work by JK Rowling. Among the many memorable moments in the saga, one of the most iconic lines is "You're a wizard, Harry".

The origin of this line comes from the first book in the series, later adapted for the screen, entitled "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone". The moment occurs when Rubeus Hagrid, Hogwarts' gamekeeper, finds young Harry at the Dursleys' house. Harry spends the early part of his life in total ignorance of his true identity. It was in this passage that Hagrid uttered the line that would change Harry's life forever.

Initiation

This line marks the beginning of a great journey for Harry Potter, leading him to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He finally discovers his heritage, his destiny, and his supernatural powers. "You are a wizard, Harry" symbolises the main character's initiation into a world of magic of which he knew nothing. This line is also the starting point for the discovery of his past, his dead parents and the fight against the evil wizard, Lord Voldemort.

Eternal magic

This sentence: "You're a wizard, Harry", goes far beyond the status of a simple line. It symbolises the entry of the most famous wizard into the wonderful world of magic. Hagrid marks the beginning of one of the most famous sagas of all time. This line is a powerful example of how a well-told story can capture the public's imagination and stand the test of time. The series has managed to leave a lasting impression far beyond the pages of the book and the cinema screen. Above all, it has left an imprint on the minds of readers and viewers alike. At last, the magic of discovery, adventure and themes that remain timeless and universal.

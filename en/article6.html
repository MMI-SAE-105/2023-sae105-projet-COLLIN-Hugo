J.K. Rowling: beyond Hogwarts, a versatile writer

J.K. Rowling, the author of Harry Potter, has created a magical world, sold 500M+ books, and sparked debate with her views on gender.

By COLLIN Hugo
Published on 18 October 2023

The ascent

JK Rowling is a world-famous author thanks to the Harry Potter series. Her real name is Joanne Rowling, and she was born on 31 July 1965 in Yate, England. In her seven books, she has succeeded in creating a world of mystery and magic that has shaken up all literature of the time and become a cultural phenomenon the world over.

Joanne Rowling struggled financially in her early years before finding success. She owed her success to her Harry Potter series of books. Her first book, Harry Potter, and the Philosopher’s Stone was the one that propelled her to the top. Before all this popularity, she wrote her first stories in Edinburgh cafes. It was in 1997, with the publication of the Harry Potter volume, that became a worldwide bestseller and propelled J.K. Rowling to stardom.

Harmony

The bestselling Harry Potter series has captivated millions of readers of all ages since 1997. The author has turned her books into a must-read series. Throughout the saga, Rowling skillfully blends universal themes such as friendship, courage, and the fight against evil with a captivating magical plot.

Commitment

But the author of the Harry Potter saga is much more than just a writer. In fact, she is a major contributor to charities and campaigns for causes such as children's rights.

Paradoxes

Nevertheless, she has not escaped controversy. Her many comments on social media have provoked debate and criticism. Indeed, Rowling is often criticised for her stance on issues of gender and identity. Despite this, she remains committed to freedom of expression and inclusion.

Legend

What's more, with over 500 million copies of the 7 Harry Potter books sold worldwide, J.K. Rowling has forged her own legacy. What's more, they have been adapted into a series of successful films. Her impact on children's literature and popular culture is immense. In short, J.K. Rowling is a writer whose work has enchanted generations and continues to do so today. Her journey from obscurity to fame is an inspiration to many writers.

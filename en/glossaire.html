Glossary

Saga

A saga is a series of stories, events, or works connected by a common storyline, central theme, or recurring characters. Sagas are often narrative and can span over extended periods, encompassing multiple books, films, episodes, etc.

Hogwarts

Hogwarts is a fictional wizarding school created by author J.K. Rowling in the "Harry Potter" book series. It is located in Great Britain, hidden from Muggles (non-magical people), and is considered one of the most prestigious magical schools in the world.

Albus Dumbledore

Albus Dumbledore is one of the central characters in the series and serves as the headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He is known for his wisdom, kindness, and powerful magical abilities.

McGonagall

She is a member of the staff at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and holds several important positions throughout the series.

Hogwarts Express

It is a train that plays a crucial role in the story as it is used by young witches and wizards to travel to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry at the beginning of each school year.

The Dursleys

The Dursleys are a family considered to be unlikable, narrow-minded, and highly anti-magic. After the death of his parents, they raise Harry Potter but treat him with disdain and neglect.

Rubeus Hagrid

Rubeus Hagrid is the Keeper of Keys and Grounds at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He quickly becomes a close friend of Harry Potter.

Nearly Headless Nick

He is a ghost residing at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and is the ghost of Gryffindor House. His name comes from the fact that he was nearly beheaded, hence the nickname "Nearly Headless."

Privet Drive

Privet Drive is a fictional address in the Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling. It is the place where the Dursley family, Harry Potter's aunt and uncle's family, resides in the early books of the series.

Gryffindor

Gryffindor is one of the four houses at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter series. This house is named after its founder, Godric Gryffindor, one of the four founders of Hogwarts.
